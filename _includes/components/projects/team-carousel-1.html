Computationally designed promoters for pig humanization

I computationally designed promoters to drive pig pan-tissue transgene expression by identifying pan-expressed genes, generating synthetic promoters using the identified sequences, and testing their activity. We initially believed the original promoters were failing due to gene silencing, but I demonstrated through experimental and computational analyses that our promoters were tissue-specific, counter prior beliefs.

Analyses of Cellular Programming in Aging

As a Sr Computational Biologist at Altos Labs, I led a matrixed team of 4 wet-lab scientists to perform translational studies of cellular programming. I developed computational pipelines to study the mechanism of action of our molecule of interest, I designed experiments to test our hypotheses and oversaw their execution.
